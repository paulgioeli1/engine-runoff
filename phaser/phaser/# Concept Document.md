# Concept Document

The purpose of this document is to provide a high level overview of the game I'm creating. The reader should be left with an idea of the overall vibe/creative direction, design pillars, core loop, value prop, and scope.


## 30 Second Ptich

Explore an Earth-like alien world that's lost its color. Idler-based farming mechanics fuel the growth of your crops, while you attract, tame, and breed beasts from across the land to solve the puzzles that gate new regions. Progress is visceral: the monochrome settings bloom their way back to color as you discover the sad mysteries of a world long left untouched, but once deeply loved.


## Design Pillars


### Pillar #1: Players have orthogonal vectors of progression, can feel them every session, and can optimize around them should they so choose.

Players are going to have at least 3 different ways to feel as though they are progressing in the game:

1. Farming - players who simply want a simple farming idler can do so, collect their crops, and turn them in against [some TBD feature] that acts as a crops sink that not simply attracting beasties
2. Taming/Raising/Breeding Beasts - players who want to tame creatures using their crops, and then raise them, use them to garner more crops, and then breed the beasts in order to develop new breeds with new traits can do so.  There will be some sort of randomization here to the genetics because, literally, that's life.
3. Puzzles - Players will be able to unlock new regions by solving intricate and clever puzzles, that are more interesting and fun then necessarily "difficult". This will allow them to open new regions, access new crops, new beasts, etc.

There might be other dimensions that I develop later, but for now, those are the three progression vectors we'll be targeting.

### Pillar #2: Players can access the game how they want to.

This is just what it sounds like. If a player wants to play on the phone for 5 minutes before bed, they can. If they want to have open on a second screen for 8 hours a day while they work as a CPA, they can. Laptopping while watching netflix, also acceptable. A nursing mother can play between babies naps. A burnt out college kid can play between study sessions. An 80 year old in a nursing home can play to pass the time. They can play for free and watch some light ads, or they can pay to have them removed.  Players can progress through purchasing things [crops? Beasts? Level unlock or puzzle hints? idk yet.]

### Pillar #3: Players should feel a sad beauty to the game: not grimdark, not popcandybubblegum aesthetic. It's the sadness of forgetting the face of someone who passed long ago, or of seeing the world change irrespective of your place in it.

Hollow knight is a sad game that echos how I want players to feel (not that I want to mirror that games aesthetic). But I want something who's overarching tone is sad or melancholy. Something like BOTW or Horizon: Zero Dawn, something that shows the player they're clearly coming in AFTER the big event, and the big event was sad, terrible, and initially made the world worse off (note these are TONAL referenes, NOT gameplaye references). I'm NOT doing pixelated grimdark, and obviously we're not going doing the mario world hyper saturated color world either. But I want an overarching curiousity to know about what happened to the world to drive players through this game.

## Core Loop


The essence of the core loop:

1. Click  mechanics help generate the first few seeds that player's can plant.
2. From there, players plant seeds. The further along the player is in the game, the greater access they have to better crops, the longer the crops take to grow.
3. Player Harvests crops.
4. Player uses crops to create items and/or to lure beasts from around the world. item creation is largely discovery based, but recipes could be found in the world.
5. Player captures/tames beast.
6. Beast comes back to farm.
7. Player uses crops and beasts to solve Color Fountain puzzles - mini puzzles throughout the world that restores the worlds color.
8. Player continues that loop to unlock ALL the color in a region (between 3 and 9 fountains) + have the right monsters and the right crops to travel to puzzle area, and uses crops and beasts to solve the puzzle.
9. Player solves puzzle unlocks new area, with new crops, beasts, maybe an npc or two, etc.
10. Player goes back to step 2, in the new region.

## World Setting / Tone

The only constant in the universe is change, and when civilizations rise, they also fall. Our players find themselves thrown into a foreign world where the remnants of an ancient civilization are still reverberating through the world. Structures overgrown with plant life, shrines that house a range of fauna, great structures now hollow are punctured with sunlight shining through a decrepit roof.
