# Concept Document

The purpose of this document is to provide a high level overview of the game I'm creating. The reader should be left with an idea of the overall vibe/creative direction, design pillars, core loop, value prop, and scope.


## 30 Second Ptich

Explore an Earth-like alien world that's lost its color. Idler-based farming mechanics fuel the growth of your crops, while you attract, tame, and breed beasts from across the land to solve the puzzles that gate new regions. Progress is visceral: the monochrome settings bloom their way back to color as you discover the sad mysteries of a world long left untouched, but once deeply loved.


## Design Pillars

I'm just gonna riff here for a second. So the big thing is that by and large we're seeing an overall degredation of competitive team based live ops. Big publishers with deep pockets are not making it work. Not even close. They all want What Riot/Blizzard/EA have, and none of them have launched games successfully.  So my design pillars are going to be based on the fact that people don't want to do that shit anymore.

Some thoughts:
I want players to feel meaningful progress every time they log on. DONE
I want players to be able to pursue depth and breadth as much as they so choose (I want a LOT of content for them to explore) DONE
I want player to be able to play "for free" with ads for as long as they want. DONE
I want this to be a really easy game to play on a second screen (phone, second display, laptop while watching netflix, whatever) Done
I want the game to be beautiful. Incredible artistic excellence.
I want there to a terribly tragic narrative throughline that unites people.
I want this to inform other creative endeavors
I don't want to punish players for being away from the game, but I DO want min/maxers to feel a light nudge to come back to optimize their idle clock (crops and beasts) Done
I want the world to feel desolate enough (at first) that when you meet an NPC it feels like a big moment, but not SO desolate that you feel like you're completely alone in the world. I also don't want it to be this idea that you have a huge village of 100 people that you're building relationships with. Think of it more like firelink shrine. There's people, you can react to them, but it's a big deal to find an NPC in the world


Alright, so if I had to distill to pillars:

- Players have orthogonal vectors of progression, and can feel them every session.
      This means they feel purposeful progression every time, and they can pursue depth and breadth as much as they want to
- Players feel minimal friction to engage how they want to.
    This means that players can play for free if they want, and they can play with whatever second screen they want doing whatever primary activty they want. When I say minimal friction, I'm refering to the feeling that min maxers should feel, that soft pressure to come back and harvest and the like.
- Players should feel a sad beauty to the game
